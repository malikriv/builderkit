# Account Warm-Up & First-Announcement Plan

A plan for warming up a Threads or X account **before** posting about LaunchThesis,
then bridging the warm audience into the first product announcement.

The whole strategy: post in the voice of the person who built LaunchThesis — a
burned indie builder who now validates ruthlessly — share that worldview, and earn
replies from your exact ICP **without naming the product**. Two weeks of that builds
the audience and credibility the launch posts later cash in.

**Voice rule (non-negotiable):** the [manifesto](../../MANIFESTO.md) voice is blunt
and anti-hype. No decorative emojis, no "🚀 excited to share." Your differentiator
is that you *won't* hype — so don't. A tool that growth-hacks dishonestly refutes its
own thesis.

---

## Warm-up post library

Pick a few, space them out. None of these name LaunchThesis.

### Confession (best opener — high relatability)
> I once spent 6 weeks building an app for an audience that, it turned out, was just me.
>
> Launched to crickets. No users, no payers, nothing.
>
> The lesson wasn't "build better." It was: I never once checked if a stranger would pay before I wrote a line of code.

### Contrarian take (drives replies)
> Unpopular opinion: a waitlist is not validation. Likes are not validation. Your friend saying "oh that's cool" is definitely not validation.
>
> The only signal that counts is a cold stranger paying you money before the thing exists.
>
> Everything else is a story you're telling yourself.

### Reframe (positions you as the smart one in the room)
> Building used to be the hard part. AI builders killed that.
>
> Now the expensive mistake isn't *can you build it* — it's spending two weeks building the wrong thing beautifully.
>
> The bottleneck moved. Most of us are still optimizing the old one.

### "No is a win" (memorable, on-brand)
> Killing a dead idea in a day should feel like a win, not a failure.
>
> The problem is nobody celebrates the app they *didn't* waste a month on. So we keep building the wrong ones.

### Question post (engagement bait, low effort, great for warm-up)
> Indie builders: what's the longest you ever spent on something that got zero users?
>
> I'll go first — 6 weeks. No judgment, we've all got one.

### Micro-thread starter (a little more reach)
> A cheap way to know if an idea is real, before you build anything:
>
> 1. Write the offer as one sentence: "this person pays this much for this thing."
> 2. Put up a page that sells it.
> 3. See if a stranger pays.
>
> If you can't make that sentence true, the app won't either.

## 2-week warm-up calendar

Rhythm: **1 post/day + 5–10 real replies/day.** Replies matter more than posts on a
cold account. Don't name LaunchThesis until Day 14.

| Day | Post type | Angle / draft seed |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Confession | The 6-weeks-for-nobody story. Sets the persona. |
| **2** | Reply-only | No post. Reply to 10 indie builders. Let Day 1 breathe. |
| **3** | Contrarian | "A waitlist is not validation." Pick a fight with comfortable beliefs. |
| **4** | Question | "Longest you spent on something with zero users?" Pure engagement. |
| **5** | Reframe | "Building used to be the hard part. AI killed that. The bottleneck moved." |
| **6** | Reply-only | Rest day for posting. Reply, quote one builder you admire. |
| **7** | Micro-thread | The 3-step "is this idea real" thread. First taste of your method. |
| **8** | "No is a win" | Nobody celebrates the app they didn't build. On-brand, memorable. |
| **9** | Question | "What's the most you'd pay, today, for [common pain]?" Surfaces WTP thinking. |
| **10** | Build-in-public tease | "I built a thing that tells me NO before I waste a month. Testing it on my own ideas this week." (Still no name.) |
| **11** | Reply-only | Engage hard. Find people complaining about failed launches — those are your people. |
| **12** | Story | A specific moment: the day you realized you'd validated nothing. Concrete > abstract. |
| **13** | Contrarian | "The builder should never be the one who decides if it worked." Plants the scoring idea. |
| **14** | **Announcement thread** | The bridge thread below. Now you name it. |

Rules baked in: never two "ask" posts back-to-back; post when your audience is awake
(test morning vs evening); screenshot anything that gets traction — that's your
proven hook for the launch.

## The bridge thread (Day 14 — first real announcement)

Built so it **only** lands because of the prior two weeks. It pays off the worldview
you've been seeding. Two platform-tuned versions follow.

>

>

>

>
> A cold stranger has to pay. Before I build. Real money, live.

> **5/**
> It's called LaunchThesis. A thesis is one sentence: "this person will pay this much for this thing."

> The tool's only job is to make that sentence true — or kill it cheap. It won't soften the signal to make you feel good.

## Platform notes

- **X:** default post limit is 280 chars (Premium raises it, but keep each thread
  post ≤ 280 so it renders for everyone). Threads of 5–7 posts perform well; lead
  post carries the hook, last post carries the CTA.
- **Threads:** 500-char limit per post. Fewer, denser posts read better than many
  short ones. Links don't suppress reach as hard as on X, but still put the CTA at
  the end.
- **Both:** a question or a confession in the first post earns early replies, which
  is what a cold account's algorithm rewards. Reply to your own thread's commenters
  fast — that's the warm-up compounding.
